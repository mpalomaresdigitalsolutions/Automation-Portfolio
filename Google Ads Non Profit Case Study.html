The Problem

The Challenge:
Flying Blind & Burning Money

Humanity Impact Institute faced critical inefficiencies in their Google Ads campaign architecture. Four systemic problems were draining budget and preventing accurate performance measurement.

🌍
Geographic Targeting Mismatch
Ads were serving to geographically irrelevant audiences — like advertising Philippine internships to people in Europe. Budget was spent on users who could never apply for the programs.
🔍
Keyword Strategy Failures
Broad-match keywords were attracting non-qualified traffic — people searching for unrelated topics who had zero intent to apply for internship programs.
📊
Flawed Conversion Tracking
The most critical flaw: tracking donations instead of internship applications. The campaign had no idea if it was actually driving the right conversions. Completely flying blind.
💸
Substantial Budget Waste
The combined effect: $847+ in wasted ad spend with no mechanism to identify what was working. Every optimization decision was made on misleading data.

Spec-Kit Execution

Optimization Framework:
8-Step Precision Methodology

A systematic Google Ads optimization process — each step builds on the last to eliminate waste, improve conversion quality, and drive measurable results for every campaign.

📱
04 — Optimize Device Bids
I break down performance by mobile, desktop, and tablet — identifying where conversions are actually happening. Devices that consistently drive leads get bid increases. Devices burning budget without conversions get bid reductions or are excluded entirely.
→ Most accounts overspend on mobile — auditing devices regularly recovers significant wasted spend
📍
05 — Optimize Location Bids
I analyze performance by city, region, and area — identifying where the lowest CPA and highest conversion rates come from. High-performing locations get bid increases to capture more of that traffic. Underperforming areas get bid reductions to stop the bleed.
→ Location data reveals hidden winners and budget drains most managers never look at
🕐
06 — Optimize Ad Schedule Bids
I identify which days of the week and hours of the day produce the most conversions at the lowest cost. Budget gets concentrated at peak conversion windows with bid adjustments — and reduced during low-conversion periods to avoid burning money when prospects aren't ready to act.
→ Ads at the right time cost less and convert more — scheduling is free budget optimization
✂️
07 — Optimize Wasteful Keywords
Converting keywords that consistently exceed the target CPA get reduced bids or are paused. Just because a keyword generates leads doesn't mean it's profitable. I monitor CPA at the individual keyword level over multiple days — patterns, not single data points, drive the decision to cut.
→ A keyword above target CPA is costing the client more per lead than it's worth — it must be addressed
👥
08 — Demographic Optimization
I layer in demographic data — age ranges, gender, and household income brackets — to refine who sees the ads. Audiences with strong conversion patterns get bid increases. Demographics with poor performance get reduced bids, reducing spend on unlikely converters and improving overall account efficiency.
→ Demographics turn a broad audience into a precision-targeted one — lowering CPL without cutting volume

This Framework in Action — Humanity Impact Institute

Applied this exact 8-step process to a broken nonprofit Google Ads account. The account was wasting budget on irrelevant traffic, tracking the wrong conversions, and serving ads to the wrong countries. Systematic layer-by-layer optimization produced measurable results across every dimension.
