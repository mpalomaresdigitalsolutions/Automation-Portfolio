# Optimization Framework: 8-Step Precision Methodology

## Overview

A systematic, layer-by-layer Google Ads optimization process. Each step builds on the last to eliminate waste, improve conversion quality, and drive measurable results for every campaign. This framework is applied weekly/bi-weekly to ensure continuous improvement.

---

## Step 1 — Search Terms Audit: Exclude Bad Keywords

**Icon:** 🔍
**Goal:** Eliminate wasted spend by blocking irrelevant searches.

### Actions

1. Navigate to **Search Terms Report** in Google Ads
2. Review every query that triggered ads in the past 7-14 days
3. Identify irrelevant, low-intent, or off-target search terms:
   - Competitor names (if not intentional)
   - Unrelated services/products
   - "Free" or "cheap" searchers (if not the target)
   - Wrong geographic locations
   - Job seekers (if running service-based campaigns)
4. Add irrelevant terms as **Negative Keywords**:
   - At **campaign level** for broad irrelevant terms
   - At **ad group level** for terms relevant to some groups but not others
5. Use **match types** for negatives:
   - Exact match: `[keyword]` for precise exclusions
   - Phrase match: `"keyword"` for variations
   - Broad match: rarely used for negatives

### Success Signal

- Impressions drop on non-converting queries
- CTR improves (waste filtered out)
- Budget reallocates to relevant searches within 24-48 hours

---

## Step 2 — Search Terms Audit: Add New High-Intent Keywords

**Icon:** ➕
**Goal:** Capture proven converting traffic not yet in your keyword lists.

### Actions

1. In the same **Search Terms Report**, filter for queries with:
   - Conversions or strong click-to-conversion ratio
   - Above-average CTR
   - Relevant intent matching your offer
2. Identify keywords **not currently in any ad group**
3. Add each new keyword to the **most relevant existing ad group**
4. Ensure the keyword matches the ad group theme:
   - Same product/service category
   - Same user intent (informational vs. transactional)
   - Same geographic targeting
5. Use appropriate match type:
   - **Phrase match** first (balance of reach and control)
   - **Exact match** for high-confidence converting terms
   - Broad match only if using Smart Bidding

### Success Signal

- New keywords start accumulating impressions and clicks
- Conversion rate aligns with existing ad group performance
- Impression share grows for proven search terms

---

## Step 3 — Ad Copy & Landing Page Alignment

**Icon:** 📊
**Goal:** Ensure keyword → ad → landing page consistency for Quality Score.

### Actions

1. For every new keyword added from Step 2, verify:
   - Ad copy contains the keyword or close variant in headlines
   - Landing page headline matches ad promise
   - Landing page content fulfills the search intent
2. If ad copy doesn't match:
   - Write new **Responsive Search Ads** with keyword-specific headlines
   - Use ad customizers for dynamic keyword insertion (when appropriate)
3. If landing page doesn't align:
   - Adjust page copy to match the keyword theme
   - Ensure CTA matches the ad's call-to-action
4. Check Quality Score before/after:
   - Keyword → ad relevance
   - Landing page experience
   - Expected CTR

### Success Signal

- Quality Score improves (typically from 4-6 to 7-10)
- CPC decreases as Quality Score improves
- Conversion rate increases due to aligned messaging

## Step 4 — Optimize Device Bids

**Icon:** 📱
**Goal:** Concentrate budget on devices that convert.

### Actions

1. Pull **Device performance report** (mobile, desktop, tablet)
2. Analyze by:
   - Conversion rate per device
   - CPA per device
   - Conversion volume per device
3. For high-performing devices:
   - Increase bid adjustment (+20-50% depending on performance gap)
4. For underperforming devices:
   - Decrease bid adjustment (-30-90% depending on waste level)
   - Consider excluding if CPA is 3x+ above target with no path to improvement
5. Monitor for 7 days after adjustment before making further changes

### Success Signal

- CPA decreases on primary converting devices
- Budget shifts toward high-conversion device segments
- Overall account efficiency improves without volume loss

---

## Step 5 — Optimize Location Bids

**Icon:** 📍
**Goal:** Prioritize geographic areas with the best conversion performance.

### Actions

1. Navigate to **Locations report** in Google Ads
2. Break down performance by:
   - City
   - Region/State
   - Metro area
   - Zip code (if available)
3. Identify top-performing locations:
   - Lowest CPA
   - Highest conversion rate
   - Most conversion volume
4. Apply **bid adjustments**:
   - +15-50% for top-performing locations
   - -20-80% for underperforming locations
   - Exclude locations with zero conversion potential
5. Monitor location performance over 14-day rolling window

### Success Signal

- CPA decreases in target markets
- Budget concentrates on areas with highest ROI
- Underperforming locations stop draining budget

---

## Step 6 — Optimize Ad Schedule Bids

**Icon:** 🕐
**Goal:** Show ads when prospects are most likely to convert.

### Actions

1. Run **Ad Schedule report** (day of week × hour of day)
2. Identify conversion patterns:
   - Peak conversion days (e.g., Tuesday-Thursday)
   - Peak conversion hours (e.g., 9 AM - 2 PM)
   - Low-performing periods (e.g., weekends at 2 AM)
3. Apply **bid adjustments by time segment**:
   - +20-40% during peak conversion windows
   - -30-70% during low-conversion periods
   - Pause ads during hours with zero conversions in 30+ days
4. Consider time zone alignment:
   - Verify campaign time zone matches target audience
   - Adjust for split time zones if targeting multiple regions

### Success Signal

- CPA improves during peak hours with increased bids
- Waste decreases during low-conversion periods
- Conversion volume maintains or increases within optimized windows

---

## Step 7 — Optimize Wasteful Keywords

**Icon:** ✂️
**Goal:** Pause or reduce bids on keywords exceeding target CPA.

### Actions

1. Review **Keywords report** sorted by CPA (highest first)
2. Identify keywords that consistently exceed target CPA:
   - Look at 14+ day performance (not single-day spikes)
   - Consider conversion volume (low-volume high-CPA terms may need different treatment)
3. For high-CPA keywords:
   - **Reduce bids** if the keyword has some conversion history (lower bid to find efficient CPA)
   - **Pause** if CPA is 2x+ above target with no improvement trend
   - **Pause** if the keyword drives no conversions in 30+ days
4. Document rationale for each pause for future reference
5. Re-evaluate paused keywords monthly (seasonal shifts may change performance)

### Success Signal

- Overall account CPA decreases toward target
- Budget reallocates from wasteful to performing keywords
- Conversion quality improves (fewer low-intent leads)

---

## Step 8 — Demographic Optimization

**Icon:** 👥
**Goal:** Refine audience targeting by age, gender, and income.

### Actions

1. Navigate to **Demographics report** in Google Ads
2. Analyze performance by:
   - Age range (18-24, 25-34, 35-44, 45-54, 55-64, 65+)
   - Gender
   - Household income (if available for the market)
   - Parental status
3. For high-performing demographics:
   - Increase bid adjustment (+10-30%)
4. For underperforming demographics:
   - Decrease bid adjustment (-20-50%)
   - Monitor for 14 days before considering exclusion
5. Combine demographic data with other signals:
   - Cross-reference with device and location performance
   - Layer demographic adjustments on top of existing bid strategies

### Success Signal

- Audience targeting becomes more precise
- CPA decreases within adjusted demographic segments
- Ad relevance improves as ads show to more receptive audiences
